Structure & Exterior
Foundation is solid — no major cracks or settling
Look for horizontal cracks, bowing walls, or uneven floors
Roof has at least 2 years of life remaining
Missing shingles, sagging, or visible wear will be flagged by appraiser
No peeling or chipping paint — interior or exterior
AUTOMATIC FHA FLAG — lead paint concern, must be remediated before closing
No broken windows or doors that won't lock/latch
Security and weatherproofing are FHA requirements
Safe access to property — driveway, walkway, entry
No major trip hazards, collapsed steps, or blocked access
Handrails on all staircases
Required on any staircase with 3+ steps
⚡
Mechanical Systems
Permanent heat source in every room
Space heaters do NOT count — must be hard-wired or ducted
No exposed or faulty wiring visible
Check basement, attic access, and older panel boxes
Hot water heater is present and up to code
Check for strapping, TPR valve, and age (10–12 yr lifespan)
Plumbing is functional — no visible leaks
Run faucets, check under sinks, look for water stains on ceilings
Stove/range is present
FHA may require stove to be present at time of appraisal
⚠️
Health & Safety — Red Flags
No visible mold or mildew anywhere
Check bathrooms, basement, crawl space, and around windows
No standing water or moisture in basement/crawl space
Smell it too — musty odor is a giveaway even without visible water
No signs of pest or termite damage
Look for mud tubes, hollow-sounding wood, or frass (termite droppings)
No unpermitted additions or structures
Additions without permits can cause appraisal and title issues
🌾
Rural & Acreage Specific
Well water — will need quality test (bacteria + nitrates)
Required for FHA — seller typically pays, budget time for results
Septic system is functional and properly distanced from well
Minimum distance varies — appraiser will flag if too close
Outbuildings/pole barns don't exceed home value
FHA lends on residential value — farm structures can complicate appraisal
Property is primarily residential — not commercial use
Mixed-use or active farm operations can disqualify FHA financing
Acreage is typical for the area — not excess farm land
FHA appraises residential portion only — large acreage may affect value
